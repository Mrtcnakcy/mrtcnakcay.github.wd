Mustafa Kemal Atatürk


Mustafa Kemal Bey (4th from right) listening to the briefing of French Colonel Auguste Edouard Hirschauer during the Picardie army manoeuvres, September 1910

Here's a time line of Mustafa Kemal Atatürk's life:


  • 1881 - Mustafa (Kemal Atatürk) born in Salonica (Selanik).
  • 1893 - Mustafa enters military preparatory school in Salonica.
  • 1905 - Passes out as Staff Captain and is posted for Fifth Army in Syria; revives a secret opposition group in Damascus.
  • 1912 - Appointed commander of Derne sector in Cyrenaica; Italo-Turkish War.
  • 1914 - Mustafa Kemal promoted Lieutenant-Colonel.
  • 1915 - Mustafa Kemal leaves Sofia to take up appointment as commander of 19th division for service in Battle of Gallipoli.
  • 1920 - Mustafa Kemal opens the Grand National Assembly (BMM) in Ankara.
  • 1922 - Gazi Mustafa Kemal begins to lead Great Offensive from the hill of Kocatepe.
  • 1928 - Speaks at Sarayburnu about the new Turkish alphabet.
  • 1934 - The Grand National Assembly of Turkey passes a law granting him the surname "ATATÜRK".
  • 1938 - Passes away.

"He was a soldier-statesman, one of the greatest leaders of our era. He ensured that Turkey got its rightful place among the most advanced nations of the world. He has given to Turks the sense of self-confidence and endurance to the Turks, that forms the foundation stone of a nation's greatness. I take great pride in being one of Atatürk's loyal friends."

-- General Douglas MACARTHUR, Commander-in-Chief of the Far East Forces, U.S.A.
